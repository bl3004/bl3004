PC gaming has evolved significantly since the 1970s. In the 1990s, improved hardware brought about a golden age with iconic titles like "Doom." The 2000s saw the rise of online multiplayer games such as "World of Warcraft." The 2010s marked an indie renaissance with smaller studios finding success on platforms like Steam. Current trends include virtual reality, improved graphics, and cloud gaming. PC gaming's significance lies in driving technological innovation, fostering creativity, and building communities. It has consistently pushed boundaries, from graphics advancements to the social aspects of esports and live-streaming.
